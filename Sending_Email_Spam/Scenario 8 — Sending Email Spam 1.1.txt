Scenario 8 — Sending Email Spam 1.1
The scenario begins at S0_Machine_Overtaken_By_Hacker_ABN, the initial situation. Unlike a typical initial state, the machine is already under adversarial control at the outset. At S0, event E1_Credentials_Stolen_Directly_ABN enters, and it is associated with two items — Provided_UserName and Provided_Password — meaning the credentials have been captured directly from the compromised machine. The threat T1_Email_Spam also enters S0, establishing the spam campaign as the objective of the takeover. Action a01_Developing_Into_ABN then leads the system to S1_User_Identity_Stolen_ABN.
At S1_User_Identity_Stolen_ABN, event E2_User_Login enters, representing the hacker logging into the victim's account using the stolen credentials. From S1, two paths diverge. The first is an intervention path: action a12_Monitoring_ABN leads to S2_System_Monitored, an abnormal terminal state in which the suspicious login activity is detected by monitoring systems, halting the campaign before it can progress further. The second path is the continuation of the attack: action a34_Duping_Into_ABN leads to S4_Email_Address_Hoaxed_ABN.
At S4_Email_Address_Hoaxed_ABN, event E3_Email_Address_Selected enters. This event is associated with two items — Person and Email_Address — representing the hacker selecting a target person and spoofing or hoaxing their email address to use as the sender or recipient of the spam campaign. Action a45_Sending_Spam_ABN then leads the system to S5_Spam_Sent_ABN.
At S5_Spam_Sent_ABN, two events enter simultaneously. The first is a second instance of E3_Update_Filters, representing a defensive response in which spam filters are updated. The second is E5_Receive_Bogus_Email_ABN, representing the target receiving the spam email. From S5, two paths diverge based on which event takes effect. If E5_Receive_Bogus_Email fires and no filter is in place, action a56_Receiving_ABN leads to the abnormal terminal state S6_Spam_Received_ABN, representing successful delivery of the spam to the target — the criminal campaign has achieved its goal. If E3_Update_Filters fires instead, action a57_Switching_Spam_Filter_On_COR leads to S7_Spam_Filter_Active, at which point the item Spam_Filter enters scope, confirming the filter has been activated. From S7, action a78_Resulting_In leads to the normal terminal state S8_Normal_State, representing successful blocking of the spam campaign.
The scenario therefore induces three named execution paths. ρ_intercepted (S0 → S1 → S2) terminates when monitoring detects the suspicious login before the campaign can proceed. ρ_criminal (S0 → S1 → S4 → S5 → S6) represents the campaign succeeding end-to-end, with spam delivered to the target. ρ_corrective (S0 → S1 → S4 → S5 → S7 → S8) represents the campaign being blocked at the point of delivery through spam filter activation. Both ρ_criminal and ρ_corrective traverse the same states up to S5, diverging only based on whether the filter update or the bogus email receipt occurs first at that branching point.
