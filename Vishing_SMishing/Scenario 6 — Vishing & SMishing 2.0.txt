**Scenario 6 — Vishing & SMishing 2.0**

The scenario begins at **S0_Normal_State**, the initial situation. At this state, three events enter simultaneously — **E1_Voice_Mail_Warning_Received_ABN**, **E2_Click_Link_ABN**, and **E3_Fake_SMS_Received_ABN** — and two threats are present: **T1_Vishing** and **T2_SMishing**. From S0, two paths immediately diverge based on which social-engineering vector the attacker deploys.

The first path is the **vishing path**, driven by T1_Vishing. Action **a01_Duping_Into_ABN** leads to **S1_Phone_Number_Hoaxed_ABN**, representing the attacker hoaxing the victim's phone number through a fraudulent voice call. At S1, two events enter. **E4_Credentials_Stolen_By_Phone_ABN** is associated with the two items **Provided_UserName** and **Provided_Password**, meaning the victim has been duped into disclosing their credentials over the phone. **E5_Login** represents the attacker subsequently using those credentials to log in. Action **a13_Logging_In** leads from S1 to **S3_Machine_Overtaken_By_Hacker_ABN**.

The second path is the **smishing path**, driven by T2_SMishing. Action **a02_Duping_Into_ABN** leads to **S2_Malicious_Link_Clicked_ABN**, representing the victim clicking a malicious link received via a fake SMS. At S2, a second instance of event **E3_Credentials_Stolen_By_Website_ABN** enters, and this event is associated with the same two items — **Provided_UserName** and **Provided_Password** — now captured through the malicious website. Action **a23_Logging_In** leads from S2 to **S3_Machine_Overtaken_By_Hacker_ABN**.

Both the vishing and smishing paths therefore converge at **S3_Machine_Overtaken_By_Hacker_ABN**. At S3, two events enter. **E6_Reinstall_OS** represents a corrective intervention. **E7_Financial_Data_Stolen_ABN** is associated with the item **Stored_Credit_Card_Details**, representing the attacker extracting stored payment card data from the overtaken machine. From S3, three paths diverge.

The first path from S3 is the corrective path: action **a36_Reinstalling_OS_COR**, triggered by **E6_Reinstall_OS**, leads to the normal terminal state **S6_OS_Normal**, representing a full system restoration before any further damage can occur.

The second path from S3 is the direct extortion path: action **a34_Extorting_ABN** leads directly to **S4_Vital_Data_Extorted_ABN**, bypassing any intermediate identity-theft step. The attacker moves immediately to the extortion stage using the already-captured data.

The third path from S3 is the identity-development path: action **a37_Developing_Into_ABN** leads to **S7_User_Identity_Stolen_ABN**, representing a deeper stage of identity appropriation. At S7, action **a74_Appropriating_ABN** leads to **S4_Vital_Data_Extorted_ABN**. Both the second and third paths therefore converge at S4.

At **S4_Vital_Data_Extorted_ABN**, event **E8_Transfer** enters. The items **AmountExtorted (xsd:int)** and **CurrentBalance (xsd:int)** are present at this state, reflecting the financial data now under the attacker's control. From S4, two paths diverge. The first is the intervention path: action **a48_Running_Analytics** leads to the corrective terminal state **S8_Transaction_Cancelled_ABN**, representing the fraudulent transfer being detected and blocked by analytics. The second is the criminal path: action **a45_Obtaining_ABN** leads to **S5_Payment_To_Criminals_ABN**, an abnormal terminal state with a self-loop. At S5, action **a4_Sending_Multiple_Payments_ABN** cycles back to S5 repeatedly, representing the attacker sending multiple fraudulent payments. The items **AmountExtorted (xsd:int)** and **CurrentBalance (xsd:int)** remain in scope across every iteration of the self-loop.

The scenario therefore induces five named execution paths. **ρ_corrective** (S0 → S1 or S2 → S3 → S6) terminates at OS reinstall before any financial damage occurs. **ρ_analytics-blocked** (S0 → S1 or S2 → S3 → S4 → S8) and its identity-theft variant (S0 → S1 or S2 → S3 → S7 → S4 → S8) both terminate at the transaction-cancelled corrective state. **ρ_criminal-direct** (S0 → S1 or S2 → S3 → S4 → S5) and **ρ_criminal-identity** (S0 → S1 or S2 → S3 → S7 → S4 → S5) both terminate at the criminal self-loop state. The two infection branches — vishing via S1 and smishing via S2 — are structurally symmetric up to S3 and produce the same downstream check-level valuations from S3 onward for all requirement checks evaluated in this scenario.
